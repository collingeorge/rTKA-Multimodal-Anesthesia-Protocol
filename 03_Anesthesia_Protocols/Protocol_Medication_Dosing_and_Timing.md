# Medication Dosing and Timing for rTKA Anesthesia

## 1. Preoperative Medications
- Acetaminophen 1 g PO — give 30–60 min pre-op; peak effect 45–60 min
- Celecoxib 400 mg PO — give 1–2 hours pre-op; 24-h coverage
- Dexamethasone 8–10 mg IV — give after IV placement; onset 10–15 min
- Scopolamine patch — “night before” OR ≥2 hours pre-op

## 2. Induction Agents (timing, onset, duration)
- Propofol 1.5–2 mg/kg IV (onset 30–45 sec; duration 5–10 min)
- Ketamine 0.3–0.5 mg/kg IV (onset <30 sec; duration 10–20 min)
- Lidocaine 1–1.5 mg/kg IV
- Rocuronium 0.6–1.0 mg/kg IV (onset 45–60 sec)

## 3. Maintenance Infusions (with titration notes)
- Dexmedetomidine 0.2–0.7 mcg/kg/h
- Ketamine 0.1–0.25 mg/kg/h
- Magnesium sulfate 30–50 mg/kg over 15–20 minutes

## 4. Emergence Timing
- Stop dexmedetomidine 10–15 minutes before extubation
- Reverse NMBD with sugammadex 2 mg/kg
- Expect wake-up in 5–10 minutes after stopping anesthetic

## 5. PACU Rescue Options
- Ketamine 0.1–0.15 mg/kg q10–15 min, max 3 doses
- Hydromorphone 0.2 mg IV PRN (opioid pathway patients)
