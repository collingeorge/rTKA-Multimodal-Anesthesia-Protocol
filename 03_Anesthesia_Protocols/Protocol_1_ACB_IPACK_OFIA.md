# Opioid-Free Intraoperative Anesthesia (OFIA) With ACB + IPACK

## 1. Purpose
Provide a standardized opioid-free intraoperative anesthesia (OFIA) protocol for robot-assisted total knee arthroplasty (rTKA) using adductor canal block (ACB) and IPACK nerve block to optimize analgesia, minimize opioid requirements, reduce PONV, and support ERAS ambulation goals.

---

## 2. Preoperative Phase

### Patient Assessment
- ASA classification, airway exam, METs, allergies.
- Review opioid tolerance history.
- Review PONV/delirium history.
- Frailty/cognition assessment for elderly.
- ASRA-compliant anticoagulation review:
  - **DOACs:** last dose ≥72 hours if CrCl > 50 mL/min  
  - **Warfarin:** INR <1.4  
  - **Prophylactic LMWH:** ≥12 hours  
  - **Therapeutic LMWH:** ≥24 hours  

### Medications (ERAS-aligned)
- **Acetaminophen 1 g PO**
- **Celecoxib 400 mg PO** (or meloxicam 15 mg if NSAID alternative needed)
- **Dexamethasone 8–10 mg IV**
- **Scopolamine 1.5 mg patch** (evening prior or ≥2 hrs pre-op)
- **Avoid gabapentinoids** (ASA/APSF 2024)

### Regional Blocks (Ultrasound-Guided)
- **Adductor Canal Block:**  
  - 20–30 mL ropivacaine **0.2–0.25%**  
- **IPACK Block:**  
  - 20 mL ropivacaine **0.2–0.25%**

Document sensory-only block (quad-sparing).

---

## 3. Induction
- Midazolam **1–2 mg IV** (anxiolysis/amnesia)
- Propofol **1.5–2 mg/kg**
- Ketamine **0.3–0.5 mg/kg**
- Dexmedetomidine loading **0.5 mcg/kg over 10–20 minutes** (optional)
- Magnesium sulfate **30–50 mg/kg over 15–20 minutes**
- Rocuronium per airway plan

---

## 4. Maintenance
- Sevoflurane or propofol infusion (TIVA)
- Dexmedetomidine **0.2–0.7 mcg/kg/hr**
- Ketamine **0.1–0.25 mg/kg/hr**
- **No intraoperative opioids**
- Maintain MAP >65 mmHg

---

## 5. Emergence
- Stop dexmedetomidine 10–15 minutes before emergence.
- Ondansetron **4 mg IV**
- Sugammadex PRN
- Smooth extubation expected due to OFIA regimen.

---

## 6. Postoperative Phase

### Scheduled Analgesia
- Acetaminophen 1 g q6h
- NSAID of choice
- **No routine opioids expected**

### Rescue Analgesia
- **Ketamine 0.1–0.15 mg/kg IV** q10–15 min PRN  
  Max 3 doses.

---

## 7. Notes
- First-line pathway for ERAS rTKA.
- Lowest nausea, best early ambulation, and least delirium.
- Ideal for opioid-naïve, elderly, or high-PONV patients.
- Midazolam provides anxiolysis without compromising opioid-free principles.
