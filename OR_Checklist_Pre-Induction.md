# Anesthesia OR Readiness & Safety Checklist  
**Validated, Clinically Accurate, Pre-Case OR Setup Guide**  
**Version 1.1 — December 2025**

---

## Purpose
A standardized, reproducible pre-case anesthesia preparation workflow ensuring equipment integrity, medication readiness, crisis preparedness, and environmental safety prior to patient arrival. This checklist promotes patient safety, reliability, and disciplined clinical habits.

---

# I. OR Environment & Station Readiness

## 1. Room & Workstation
- Confirm correct OR assignment.
- Room temperature appropriate for patient type.
- Table positioned and locked; safety straps available.
- Suction functioning with Yankauer.
- Overhead lights functional.
- Waste and sharps containers present and not overfilled.

## 2. Anesthesia Machine (ASA/APSF Checkout)
- Power on; complete automated self-test.
- Verify:
  - Circuit integrity (leak test)  
  - APL valve function  
  - Reservoir bag integrity  
  - **Two oxygen sources:** pipeline + **full E-cylinder (≥ 1800–2200 psi preferred)**  
  - Face mask seal  
  - CO₂ absorber status  
- Vaporizers filled and locked.
- Fresh gas flows at safe baseline.

## 3. Airway Equipment
- Laryngoscope handles with charged batteries.
- Blades: Mac/Miller appropriate sizes.
- Video laryngoscope tested.
- Oral and nasal airways.
- Endotracheal tubes (primary + size up/down).
- Supraglottic airway device (LMA/iGel).
- Stylet, bougie, tube exchanger.
- Syringe for cuff inflation.
- ETCO₂ line tested.
- Difficult airway cart location verified.

---

# II. IV Access, Fluids & Blood Prep

## 4. IV Access
- Primary IV catheter prepared and flushed.
- Secondary IV/rapid line if anticipated.
- Pressure bags available and tested.

## 5. Fluids
- Balanced crystalloid spiked and ready.
- Additional fluids:
  - Colloid  
  - Blood products (if indicated)  
  - Rapid infuser availability if needed  

## 6. Blood & Labs
- Type & Screen / Crossmatch verified.
- Blood units available if required.
- Critical labs reviewed: H/H, electrolytes, renal panel, glucose, pregnancy test.

---

# III. Medication Setup (Safety-Validated)

## 7. Induction Medications (Drawn & Labeled)
- Induction agent: propofol/etomidate/ketamine.
- **Opioid-sparing or opioid-free pathway selected**.
- Neuromuscular blocker: roc/vec/sux.

## 8. Emergency Drugs (Immediate Access)
- **Epinephrine 10 mcg/mL (push-dose) + 1 mg crash vial**.
- Phenylephrine.
- Ephedrine.
- Atropine and glycopyrrolate.
- Vasopressin (if institutionally allowed).
- Local anesthetic for airway topicalization.

## 9. Adjuvant / Maintenance Meds
- Antiemetics.
- Antibiotics (timing confirmed).
- Multimodal analgesics.
- Steroids (if indicated).
- Infusions prepared: remifentanil, propofol, ketamine, lidocaine, dexmedetomidine, esmolol, clevidipine.

**All syringes labeled with medication, concentration, date, and initials.**

---

# IV. Airway & Ventilator Safety

## 10. Pre-Oxygenation Setup
- Circuit connected and functioning.
- ETCO₂ waveform confirmed.
- Oxygen flow verified.
- Mask seal checked.

## 11. Ventilator Settings
- Initial ventilator mode selected.
- Verify alarms:
  - High pressure  
  - Low pressure  
  - Low tidal volume  
  - Apnea  
- FiO₂ appropriate for induction.

---

# V. Monitoring & Documentation

## 12. Standard ASA Monitors
- ECG.
- **NIBP cuff correct size on contralateral arm or leg**.
- Pulse oximeter (primary + ear/forehead backup).
- Capnography.
- Temperature probe.
- Neuromuscular monitoring (TOF).
- BIS/depth monitor if applicable.

## 13. Special Monitors
- Arterial line setup.
- Central venous kit.
- Cardiac output/PPV monitoring.
- Ultrasound machine with sterile covers.

## 14. Documentation
- EMR anesthesia record open.
- Consent verified.
- Allergies reviewed.
- Surgical site and timeout information confirmed.

---

# VI. Regional Anesthesia Prep (If Applicable)

## 15. Block Setup
- Ultrasound machine operational.
- Block tray prepared.
- Sterile drapes, gloves, probe cover.
- Needles and catheters.
- Local anesthetic labeled; dose limits checked.
- Lipid emulsion (20%) available immediately.

---

# VII. Crisis & Emergency Preparedness

## 16. Airway Rescue Ready
- Supraglottic airway device ready.
- Video laryngoscope functional.
- Bougie at bedside.
- Suction on.

## 17. Hemodynamic Rescue
- Push-dose pressors ready.
- Vasopressors prepared or immediately available.
- Fluids ready.
- Rapid infuser availability confirmed for high-risk cases.

## 18. Code & Safety Equipment
- Defibrillator location verified.
- Code cart nearby.
- **Malignant hyperthermia cart location verified + dantrolene available.**
- POCUS availability confirmed.

---

# VIII. Team Communication & Surgical Coordination

## 19. Pre-Induction Huddle (Surgeon, CRNA/Anesthesiologist, Circulator)
Discuss:
- Anesthesia pathway: opioid-free, opioid-sparing, or conventional.
- Airway plan and anticipated difficulty.
- Positioning and padding strategy.
- Anticipated blood loss.
- Antibiotic timing.
- Pain management plan.
- PACU/ICU disposition.
- **Critical lab or echo findings (severe AS, low EF, pulmonary HTN).**
- Special equipment or implants.

---

# IX. Final Check Before Patient Enters the OR

## 20. “Wheels-In” Verification
- Machine OK.
- Monitors OK.
- Medications labeled.
- Airway tray fully stocked.
- Suction functional.
- Fluids available.
- Documentation ready.
- Entire team aware and prepared.
