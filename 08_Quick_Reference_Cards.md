# Quick Reference Cards
Robot-Assisted Total Knee Arthroplasty (rTKA) – 2025

Print → laminate → pocket / anesthesia machine

| Pathway 1 – Preferred | OFIA + ACB/IPACK (Opioid-Free + Blocks) |
|---|---|
| **Pre-op** | • Acetaminophen 1 g PO/IV<br>• Celecoxib 400 mg PO (or institutional NSAID plan; avoid stacking NSAIDs)<br>• Dexamethasone 8–10 mg IV<br>• Scopolamine patch (high PONV risk; per policy) |
| **Blocks (Pre-Induction)** | • **Midazolam 1–2 mg IV** (consider 0.5–1 mg in elderly/OSA)<br>• ACB: ropivacaine 0.2–0.25%, 20–30 mL<br>• IPACK: ropivacaine 0.2–0.25%, 15–20 mL<br>• Document sensory coverage + motor-sparing (quad strength) |
| **Induction** | • Propofol 1.5–2 mg/kg<br>• Ketamine 0.3–0.5 mg/kg<br>• Rocuronium 0.6–1.0 mg/kg<br>• Additional midazolam 0.5–1 mg IV **only if needed** (titrate to effect) |
| **Adjuncts** | • Magnesium sulfate 30–50 mg/kg (typical ~40 mg/kg) over 15–20 min<br>• Dexmedetomidine 0.2–0.7 mcg/kg/hr (typical 0.2–0.6 in age >70/frail; avoid loading dose in high-risk) |
| **Maintenance** | • **Sevoflurane ~0.8–1.2 MAC** OR **Propofol TIVA 75–150 mcg/kg/min**<br>• Ketamine 0.1–0.25 mg/kg/hr<br>• Dexmedetomidine (as above)<br>• **Maintain MAP ≥65 mmHg (individualize)** |
| **Emergence** | • Stop dex 10–15 min prior (as tolerated)<br>• Ondansetron 4 mg IV<br>• Sugammadex **2 mg/kg (TOF ≥2)** or **4 mg/kg (deep block)** |
| **PACU Rescue** | • Expected pain NRS 0–3/10<br>• **Ketamine 0.1–0.15 mg/kg IV** slow push q10–15 min (max 3 doses)<br>• If still NRS ≥4 after 3 doses → **hydromorphone 0.2 mg IV** q10–15 min PRN *(high-risk elderly/OSA/frail: start 0.1 mg)* |

## Universal Safety Reminders (All Pathways)

### Standard Medications
- **Midazolam 1–2 mg IV** is standard anxiolysis and compatible with OFIA (titrate; reduce in elderly/OSA).
- **Sevoflurane** and **propofol TIVA** are both acceptable maintenance strategies; choose based on patient factors and clinician judgment.

### Critical Safety Points
- **Avoid routine gabapentinoids** in ERAS-era pathways due to sedation/delirium/respiratory risk (follow institutional policy).
- **Lipid emulsion 20%** immediately available (regional anesthesia / LAST preparedness).
- **Dexmedetomidine bradycardia:** if symptomatic and HR <50 → stop/decrease infusion; consider **glycopyrrolate 0.2–0.4 mg IV** per clinician judgment.
- **Local anesthetic safety:** document total local anesthetic dose (ACB + IPACK + infiltration) and patient weight; monitor for LAST symptoms.

### PACU Monitoring Triggers (Practical Cutoffs)
Consider enhanced monitoring / escalation if:
- **High cumulative opioid exposure** (e.g., **≥2 mg IV hydromorphone OR ≥300 mcg IV fentanyl** total PACU/OR), or
- OSA (STOP-Bang ≥3), age >70, frailty, baseline cognitive impairment, or concurrent sedatives.

### ASRA/ESRA 2024–2025 (Blocks / Neuraxial)
- Timing is **agent-, dose-, renal-function-, and procedure-dependent**. Always follow the **current ASRA/ESRA guidance + institutional policy** for neuraxial and deep peripheral blocks.
