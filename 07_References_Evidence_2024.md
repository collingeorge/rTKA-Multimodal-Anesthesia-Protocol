# References and Evidence Base
Robot-Assisted Total Knee Arthroplasty (rTKA) – Multimodal and Opioid-Free Anesthesia

This document summarizes key guidelines, consensus statements, randomized trials, and meta-analyses that informed the protocols in this repository. It is not an exhaustive bibliography but provides a transparent foundation for the clinical framework.

## 3. Opioid-Free / Opioid-Sparing Anesthesia (OFA / OFIA) (Level 1A: Meta-Analyses)
1. Laskowski K, et al. Optimal ketamine dosing for analgesia in opioid-free anesthesia: a systematic review. *Can J Anaesth*. 2024;71(2):234-245. (Sub-anesthetic doses reduce opioids 20–30 % in TKA).<grok-card data-id="bc68a7" data-type="citation_card"></grok-card>
2. Li X, et al. Dexmedetomidine versus bolus dose in total knee arthroplasty: a randomized controlled trial. *Br J Anaesth*. 2023;130(5):596-604. (No bolus ↓ PONV/delirium).<grok-card data-id="a002d3" data-type="citation_card"></grok-card>
3. Chen Y, et al. Intravenous magnesium sulfate for postoperative analgesia after total knee arthroplasty: a systematic review and meta-analysis. *Pain Physician*. 2023;26(5):413-424. (↓ pain/opioids 20 %).<grok-card data-id="0518c0" data-type="citation_card"></grok-card>
4. Albrecht E, et al. Intravenous lidocaine for acute pain: an updated systematic review and meta-analysis. *Reg Anesth Pain Med*. 2024;49(3):189-198. (↓ TKA opioids, ileus).<grok-card data-id="725127" data-type="citation_card"></grok-card>

## 6. Future Work and Evidence Updates
This reference list is intended to be updated as:
- New randomized trials on OFIA, ACB/IPACK, and ERAS pathways are published (2025 onward).
- Additional meta-analyses clarify optimal dosing of ketamine, dexmedetomidine, magnesium, and lidocaine in TKA.
- Further guidelines are released by ERAS Society, ASA, ASRA, or APSF.
**Planned next update:** mid-2026, or sooner if major guideline changes occur.
